## Summary

The amount of investment of capital will evi- dently be determined by multiplying each por- tion of capital invested at any moment by the length of time for which it remains invested. We may obtain a general expression for the rate of interest yielded by capital in any employment provided that we may suppose the produce for the same amount of labour to vary as some continuous function of the time elapsing between the expen- diture of the labour and the enjoyment of the result. If the soil yields much, and capital will make it yield more, then both wages and interest will be high ; if the soil yields much, and capital will not make it yield more, then wages will be high and interest low, unless the capital finds other investments.
