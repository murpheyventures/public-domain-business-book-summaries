## Summary

THE DOLLARS AND CENTS OF ADVERTISING BY MOTION PICTURES So far as I am aware, the cost aspects of advertising by motion pictures have not been dealt with in print before. As in all things, the cost is the deciding point, and although the average advertiser will not quibble over a few dollars where there is the prospect of increased business, he, nevertheless, likes to know beforehand just what the campaign is going to cost. I will suppose you follow the vade mecum of most advertisers and arrange to have a single-reel motion picture produced showing conditions at your plant. Providing sufficient daylight is available, the usual inclusive fee charged for the producing and developing of such a film is fifty cents per foot--or $500 for the entire reel, which is exactly a thousand feet.
