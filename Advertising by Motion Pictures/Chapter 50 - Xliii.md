## Summary

HOW THE BOOK DEALER CAN TAKE ADVANTAGE OF THE MOVIE ADAPTATION MANIA The saloon-keeper may attribute decreased business to the versatile motion picture, but to the average book dealer the industry can be most beneficial. This has been followed up by many other adaptations from novels and stage plays, and in every case it has meant extra trade for the book dealer who has been keen enough to make good use of the opportunities thus presented. Instead of borrowing the book from the local library, they prefer to spend up to a quarter on a cheap edition--and this is precisely where the book trade comes in. The exhibitor could inform the book dealer well in advance whether he had any adaptations booked, so that the book dealer could lay in a stock to meet the demand.
