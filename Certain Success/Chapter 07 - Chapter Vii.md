## Summary

The ego is the master, not the slave, of the body. [Sidenote: True Signs of Character] _The true signs of character are to be read only in the words, tones, and movements_ of a man--and in his muscle structure _as he has developed it_ or has left it _undeveloped_. But should there be a moment or two of delay before he picks up the pencil, his _slower physical response_ to your request is to be read as an _indication that his mind does not grasp ideas at once_. [Sidenote: Keep Mental Pace] After making your size-up of the degree of his mental speed, you can govern your presentation by what you have learned. Should you say something with a touch of humor, the time it takes him to smile or twinkle his eyes will measure the speed of his mind in catching ideas. [Sidenote: Head and Eye Movements] The movements of the head and of the eyes, according to which are predominant in the case of an individual, tell much of his character. Your eyes and ears can take in specific points about your prospect without making their keen activity apparent. [Sidenote: Two Parts of Sizing-up Process] When you have learned how to see and hear many details clearly at the same time, _unsuspected by your prospect_, you will be a master of the first essential of skillful character reading.
