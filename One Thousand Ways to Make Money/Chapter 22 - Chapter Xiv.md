## Summary

ALUMINUM, THE NEW MINERAL.--“The product of aluminum in the United States,” says a mining expert, “should be three million pounds in 1900.” The present price is from thirty-five to fifty cents per pound. NORTH CAROLINA MICA.--In the mountains of North Carolina are found the best mica dikes in the United States, but the methods of mining are crude and bring small profit. AFRICAN DIAMONDS.--Diamonds in vast numbers are found in the beds of many South African streams, but if you have capital you may develop an industry like that of the De Beers Company, which is paying forty per cent. per annum. It is believed that if these mines were bored deeper, potassium salt--a salt hitherto not found in the United States--would be discovered, and home plants take the place of foreign imports.
