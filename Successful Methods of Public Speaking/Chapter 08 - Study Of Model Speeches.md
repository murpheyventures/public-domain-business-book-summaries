## Summary

Carefully note any special means employed--story, illustration, appeal, or climax,--to increase the effectiveness of the speech. _John Stuart Mill_ Read the following speech delivered by John Stuart Mill, in his tribute to Garrison. A noble work nobly done always contains in itself not one but many lessons; and in the case of him whose character and deeds we are here to commemorate, two may be singled out specially deserving to be laid to heart by all who would wish to leave the world better than they found it. As you compare one speech with another, you will more readily see why each subject requires a different form of treatment, and also learn to judge how the speaker has availed himself of the possibilities afforded him. _Judge Story_ The speech which follows is a fine example of elevated and impassioned oratory. Death never comes too soon, if necessary, in defense of the liberties of our country." You can advantageously read aloud many times a speech like the foregoing.
