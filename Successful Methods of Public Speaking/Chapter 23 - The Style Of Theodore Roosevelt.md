## Summary

Roosevelt commend themselves to the student of public speaking for their fearlessness, frankness, and robustness of thought. His style was generally exuberant, and the note of personal assertion prominent. He was direct in diction, often vehement in feeling, and one of his characteristics was a visible satisfaction when he drove home a special thought to his hearers. Roosevelt's famous address, "The Strenuous Life," will lead the student to study the speech in its entirety.
