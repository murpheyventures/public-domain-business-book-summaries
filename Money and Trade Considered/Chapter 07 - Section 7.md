## Summary

To explain this matter better, I shall suppose when money is raised, goods rise, or not. It may be alledged, we have more product and manufacture, than is consumed, or exported; and selling cheaper, would occasion a greater demand for our goods abroad. Some think foreign money being raised, would bring in money to Scotland. If all import, and foreign expence were discharged, Scotland would then be so much richer, as there was bullion or money imported: but, if that prohibition be supposed, Scotland would be richer by keeping the money at the value it has; because, a greater quantity would be brought in, to buy the same quantity of goods.
