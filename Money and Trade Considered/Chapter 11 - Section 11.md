## Summary

Tho’ money or silver is so much fallen from the value it had, yet it’s given as a value for one half, or two thirds more than its value as silver, abstract from its use as money. Suppose silver to be no more used as money in Europe, its quantity would be the same, and the demand for it much lesser; which might lower it 2 thirds or more; for besides that the demand would be less, its uses as plate, &c. are not near so necessary, as that of money. The same answer may be given to these who think an addition to the money of any particular country would undervalue it so, that the same quantity of goods would cost double the money as before. 3dly, That tho’ fallen so much, yet it is given as money or sold as bullion, for much more than its value as a metal; to which it will be reduced, so soon as another money is set up.
