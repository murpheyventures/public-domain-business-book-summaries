## Summary

Then the price of the services rendered by the stones would have been governed by the natural scarcity of the aggregate output of their services in relation to the demand for those services; and the aggregate surplus or rent would most easily be reckoned as the excess of this scarcity price over the aggregate expenses of working the stones. For, if the bad land were to be flooded and reXof he rendered incapable of producing anything at all, the cultivation of superior, other land would need to be more intensive; and therefore the price of the product would be higher, and rents generally would be higher, than if that land had been a poor contributor to the total stock of produce . Thus it has been said that a quasi-rent is an “ unnecessary profit,” and that it is “ no part of cost.” Quasi -rent is correctly described as an unnecessary profit in regard to short periods, because no “ special ” or “ prime ” costs have to be incurred for the production of a machine that, by hypothesis, is already made and waiting for its work. The cost of production on the margin of the profitable application of capital and labour is that to which the price of the whole produce tends, under the control of the general conditions of demand and supply : it does not govern price, but it focuses the causes which do govern price. § 2.
