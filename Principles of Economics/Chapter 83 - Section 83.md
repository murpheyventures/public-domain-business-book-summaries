## Summary

The owner of building land sometimes builds on it himself : causoB sometimes he sells it outright : very often he lets it at a fixed ground- rent for ninety-nine years, after which the land and the buildings on the oapitai it (which by covenant must be kept in good repair) revert to his building successor in title. In places where the value of land is high, each square foot is made to yield perhaps twice the accommo- dation, at more than twice the cost, that it would be made to give, if used for similar purposes where the value of land is low. The general relations of demand and supply cause production to be carried up to a margin at which the expenses of production (nothing being entered for rent) are so high that people are willing to pay a high value for additional land in order to avoid the inconvenience and expense of crowding their work on to a narrow site. Again, a hop-grower may find that on account of the high rent which he pays for his land, the price of his hops will not cover their expenses of production where he is, and he may abandon hop-growing, or seek other land for it ; while the land that he leaves may perhaps be let to a market-gardener.
