- And a formal definition of the demand curve for a market may be given thus : — The demand curve for any commodity in a market during any given unit of time is the locus of demand points for it.

- If it is slow the price that he will give for the commodity will not fall much in consequence of a con- siderable increase in his supply of it ; and a small fall in price will cause a comparatively large increase in his purchases.
- And we may say generally : — The elasticity (or responsive- ness) of demand in a market is great or small according as the amount demanded increases much or little for a given fall in price, and diminishes much or little for a given rise in price.
