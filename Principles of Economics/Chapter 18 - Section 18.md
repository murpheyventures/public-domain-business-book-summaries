## Summary

The language of common life while averse to so broad a use of the term Income as this even when discussing social problems, yet habitually in- cludes a certain number of forms of income, other than money income. The Income Tax Commissioners count a dwelling-house inhabited by its owner as a source of taxable income, though it yields its income of comfort directly. And almost every use of the term capital, which is known to history, has corresponded more or less closely to a parallel use of the term Income : in almost every use, capital has been that part of a man’s stock from which he expects to derive an income. And this is an additional reason for making the terms Capital and Income correlative from the social, as we did from the individual point of view.
