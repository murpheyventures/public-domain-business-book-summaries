## Summary

As the price required to attract purchasers for any given amount of a commodity, was called the demand price for that amount during a year or any other given time ; so the price required to call forth the exertion necessary for producing any given amount of a commodity, may be called the supply price for that amount during the same time. The requisites of production are commonly spoken of as land, labour and capital : those material things which owe their usefulness to human labour being classed under capital, and those which owe nothing to it being classed as land. The action of fresh air and water and of frosts are nature’s tillage of the soil ; and even unaided they will in time make almost any part of the earth’s surface fairly fertile if the soil that they form can rest where it is, and is not torn away down-hill by rain and torrents as soon as it is formed.
