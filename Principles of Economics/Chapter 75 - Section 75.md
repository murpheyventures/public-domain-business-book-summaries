## Summary

To substitute cost of reproduc- tion for cost of production in the theory of normal values is to make no real change ; and though the market value of a thing is sometimes nearer cost of re- insurance against risk; though there may be something to be allowed as a charge on account of uncertainty. Carey, observing this, suggested that we should speak of value in relation to (money) cost of reproduction instead of in relation to cost of production. For normal cost of production and normal cost of reproduction are convertible terms ; and no real change is made by saying that the normal value of a thing tends to equal its normal (money) cost of reproduction instead of its normal (money) cost of production. And no valid argument for the change can be founded on the fact, which may be readily admitted, that there are some few cases in which the market value of a thing is nearer its cost of reproduction than the cost that was actually incurred in producing that particular thing.
