## Key Findings

- In the preceding chapter we were engaged in a study of the differences between the marginal social net product and the marginal trade net product of resources devoted to various occupations or industries.
- The indirect and ultimate services, which either will render, have, as a general rule, little or no weight in the balance." ^ These indirect services constitute the difference between the social net product and the trade net product of a unit of resources invested in any form of economic organisa- tion.
- 182 ECONOMICS OF WELFAKE pt. h ship workshops constitute an industrial form, investment in which is likely to yield a marginal social net product considerably in excess of the marginal trade net product.
- That element of social net product, however, does not accrue to those persons by whom the size of agricultural holdings is regulated, and is not included in the marginal trade net product of the resources invested in them.
- The same line of thought, looked at from the other side, suggests that the marginal social net product of activities devoted to bringing about any widespread " trustification " of industry is likely to be smaller than the marginal trade net product.
