## Summary

When the method of engagement of labour is either the privileged class or the casual method, the effect on the national dividend is different, according as the demand for labour, in the place or occupation where the wage rate is raised, has an elasticity less than, or greater than, unity. Where the elasticity of demand is so great that the raising of the wage rate to the " fair " level reduces the demand for labour to zero, the attractiveness of the place or occupation to all workpeople is also reduced to zero, and those assembled at it must, there- fore, move away. Let PM be the wage that would result from free competition and is equal to the general rate of wages for workpeople of the grade concerned ; QM" the wage most profitable to the workpeople if they were combined ; and RM' the wage most profitable to the employers. If the work- people succeeded in establishing a wage larger than PM, the exchange index would necessarily fall on the demand curve to the left of P, say at the point Q, and we might speak of an exploitation of employers by workpeople measured by the excess of QM" over FM".

- The value of the marginal net product of labour of given quality would be the same to all employers, and, if one employer offered a man less than others, that man would know that he could at once get as much as this value of his marginal net product from others.
