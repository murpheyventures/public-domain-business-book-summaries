## Summary

An adjustment is brought about, either by employers taking on inferior men at the wage formerly paid to good men only,^ or by resort to overtime at special rates. * It may be suggested that under a piecework system this device is impracticable, since a given wage bears the same relation to a given output, whoever the worker may be. From a combination of these results it follows that, over good and bad times together, a wage system fluctuating on both sides of the mean level in accordance with temporary movements of the demand for labour means more work and, therefore, a larger national dividend than one permanently fixed at that level. 521. cH.xvni FIXED & FLUCTUATmG WAGE EATES 563 and considerable alterations." ^ If this is true, it follows that the direct advantages of a wage rate fluctuating with fluctuations of demand may be more than counteracted by indirect disadvantages.
