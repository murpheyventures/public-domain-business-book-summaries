## Summary

In occupations in which 110 resources are employed, the value of the marginal net product of resources will, in general, be smaller than it is in occupations where same resources are employed. We cannot say that, when the costs of movement are equivalent to n shillings, the national dividend is best served by a distribution under which the values of the marginal net products at A and B differ by such and such a defined number of shillings.
