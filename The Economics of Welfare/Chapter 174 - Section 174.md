## Key Findings

- The other constituent is demanded (or supplied) by the several sources in such a way that the demand (or supply) price in each source depends solely upon the amount in that source.
- In applying it we are, it must be granted, ignoring the fact that the effect on the supply price of the r^^ source of supply brought about by a given change in the output of that source may itself be diiferent, according as the aggregate output of the whole market is large or small.
- Let it be supposed that we have to do with a unified source of demand and with two sources of supply which are entirely independent, so that the aggregate supply curve is made up by a simple addition of their separate supply curves.
- It follows that, other things being equal, when one source of supply is taxed and the other left free, the rise of price will be greater, the greater are the output and elasticity of the taxed source relatively to the untaxed source.
- From this result it is an easily deduced corollary that, when the choice is, not between the taxation of one source and of both sources, but between the taxation of one source and of the other source, price will be raised less if the source in which the supply is less elastic is selected. § 15.
