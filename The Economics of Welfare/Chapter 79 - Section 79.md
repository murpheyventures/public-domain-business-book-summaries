## Summary

Since the workpeople's sticking point may be anything below the highest point in the range of indetermin- ateness, it may be below the lowest point in that range. In these circumstances the range of practicable bargains will not extend to the two sticking points : for no bargain is practicable that lies outside the range of inde- terminateness. The range of ■practic- able bargains, that is the series of wage rates which both parties would prefer to a fight, is constituted by the wage rates (if any) which both lie above the workpeople's sticking point and below the employers' sticking point and also fall within the range of indeteiminateness. Within the limits set by the range of indeterminateness it will, therefore, extend the range of practicable bargains in both directions, provided that such range already exists, and it may bring such a range into being if none exists.

## Key Findings

- Since the workpeople's sticking point may be anything below the highest point in the range of indetermin- ateness, it may be below the lowest point in that range.
- In these circumstances the range of practicable bargains will not extend to the two sticking points : for no bargain is practicable that lies outside the range of inde- terminateness.
- The range of ■practic- able bargains, that is the series of wage rates which both parties would prefer to a fight, is constituted by the wage rates (if any) which both lie above the workpeople's sticking point and below the employers' sticking point and also fall within the range of indeteiminateness.
- Within the limits set by the range of indeterminateness it will, therefore, extend the range of practicable bargains in both directions, provided that such range already exists, and it may bring such a range into being if none exists.
- If, for instance, the lower end of the range of practicable bargains is already lying at the lower end of the range of indeterminateness, the introduction of an arbitration agreement cannot extend the range of practicable bargains in a sense favourable to employers, while it may extend it in a sense adverse to them.
