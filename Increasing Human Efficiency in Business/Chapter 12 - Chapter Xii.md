## Summary

In strictness what is capitalized is not the practical judgment itself but the original concrete experience that is recalled at a later time, and upon the basis of which a practical judgment is formed. A much more effective form of thought is the formation of reflective judgments. _Reflective judgments_ A practical judgment is based on a single concrete case. I had no preformed practical judgment which I could apply to the case and did not even possess a remembrance of any experience upon which I might base a practical experience. We find that four classes of experiences are valuable, _i.e_. such experiences as result in the <p 296> formation of habits; such as result in practical judgments, in reflective judgments, and in expert judgments.
