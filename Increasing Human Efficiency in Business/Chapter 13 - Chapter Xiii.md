The folly of such a criticism of habit is made apparent by the study of any act which may be performed by one person as a habit and by another person as an act every step of which demands attention. We are also inclined to deprecate habits on the ground that the man in the grip of habit is hopelessly in the _*rut_, that the man who has reduced his work to habit ceases to be original and is incapable of further improvement.
