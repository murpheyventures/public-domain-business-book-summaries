## Summary

Young, would be open to insuper- able objections. At particular times it might be the means of converting a dearth into a famine. If however this adjustment instead of being enforced by law, were produced by the increasing operation of the prudential check to marriage, the effect would be to- tally different, and in the highest degree beneficial to so- ciety. A peck of wheat a day cannot be considered in any light as excessive wages.
