## Summary

I trust that I am disposed to attach as much importance to the effects of morality and religion on the happiness of society, even as Mr. Weyland ; but among the moral duties, I certainly include a restraint upon the inclination to an early mar- riage when there is no reasonable prospect of mainte- nance for a family ; and unless this species of virtuous self-denial be included in morality, I am quite at issue with Mr. Weyland ; and so distinctly deny his proposi- tion as to say that no degree of religion and morality, no degree oi rational liberty and security of person and pro- perty, can, under the existing laws of nature, place the lower classes of society in a state of comfort and plen- ty.
