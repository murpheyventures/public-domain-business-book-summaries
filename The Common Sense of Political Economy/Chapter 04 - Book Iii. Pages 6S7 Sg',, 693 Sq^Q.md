## Summary

However high his ^ remuneration is, it cannot be fixed by the economic forces tend forces any higher than the estimated worth of Ms services at the margin ; and, however low it may as much as be, it cannot be held down by those economic forces any lower than that marginal worth. At every stage the cost incurred in making a thing is the relinquished possibility of making other things, and its extent or amount is determined by the value, or marginal significance on the collective scale, which those other things would have had. The marginal significance of things that can no longer be produced instead of it has no effect on its present price ; the marginal significance of things that can still be produced instead of it will determine the lower limit of the price at which it will be made to order, and the extent to which manufacturers will continue to make it at all.
