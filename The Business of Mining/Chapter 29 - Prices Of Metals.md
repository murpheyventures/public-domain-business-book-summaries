## Summary

This very peculiar value is known as the "mint value," and is the price which the Government of the United States pays for all of its coinage gold. Most all the gold produced in the world is associated with other metals, such as silver, copper, or platinum, so that the bullion recovered in milling or smelting will usually contain the gold alloyed with such other metals and the gold is said to be not "fine," or pure. Silver has a fluctuating market value although attempts have been made, at times, to establish its value at some fixed ratio to the value of gold. The schedule of prices announced in April, 1912, for Boulder County ores and concentrates provides as follows, a unit being understood to mean 1 per cent or 20 pounds per ton: For material assaying 10 per cent WO_{3}, $3.50 per unit; for 20 per cent WO_{3}, $4.40 per unit; for 40 per cent and more, $4.90 per unit.
