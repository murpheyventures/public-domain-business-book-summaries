## Summary

The greatest development of this sort of mining has come about since the application of the modern steam shovel to the excavation of ore. There are about a dozen mines of this "open pit" type that have each produced over a million tons of ore per year in a season that must cease with the close of navigation on the Great Lakes. At the Utah Copper Company's mine in Bingham Canyon, Utah, a great deposit of low grade, copper-bearing eruptive rock is being handled upon a steep mountain-side by this same scheme. This ore averages a little less than two per cent. in copper, but so economical is the handling of it in such vast amounts that a neat profit is made above all mining, transportation and milling charges.
