## Summary

Said the Butler County Democrat: "It was simply a question whether Pendleton, Ward, Thurman, Converse, Follett, Geddes, or any other capable and honest Demo- crat, should receive the compliments of a seat in the Senate, or that the Standard Oil Company should buy the place for Henry B. It was an honest and divided Democracy against a hydra-headed dictatorship of rich men on whose banner was inscribed 'Money Talks.'" The Carroll County Chronicle in commenting on the election said: "It is a great mistake to suppose Standard Oil has captured the Democratic party of Ohio. When the British got General Benedict Arnold they imagined they had captured the United States army, but it was a mistake." "The monopoly of the Standard Oil Company must be destroyed," declared the Columbus Times. We sincerely trust that the investigation, which is as much demanded for the honour of the sitting members as for that of the Senate or the state of Ohio, may result in vindicating his title to his seat and the good name of the Legis- lature that elected him. * Report Number 1490, United States Senate, Forty-ninth Congress.
