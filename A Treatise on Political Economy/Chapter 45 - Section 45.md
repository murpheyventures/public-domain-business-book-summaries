- Their personal motive was, on all such occasions, to pay less, or receive more silver or gold themselves, than in honesty they ought ; but they sometimes compelled individuals, notwithstanding the alteration, to pay and receive in the old coin, or, if in the new, at the current rate of exchange between the two. * This was a close copy of a Roman precedent.
