## Summary

I admit, that the ignorance and vices of her rulers for the time being have made her pay for those products much dearer, than she need have done : but now that she buys them at the natural price, (exclusive of course of the import duties,) and pays for them as before with her domestic products, in what way is she a loser? I. confined to Nantes and Bourdeaux ; and those cities have suifered in consequence- But, as France now consumes at least as much of those articles as ever she did^ all, that has not come by the way of Nantes or Bourdeaux, must needs have found its way in some other channel. The nation, therefore, that should have no share of territories so highly favored by na- ture, would lie at the mercy of the nation, that should first get possession ; for the monopoly of purchasing the colonisd produce would enable her to exact her own price from her less for- tunate neighbour. All that the nation gains is, the profit upon its dealings with him, and upon what he purchases ; and this is by no means contemptible, for every extension of commerce is a proportionate advantage*; but it is well to know its real amount, that we may not be betrayed into the folly of pur- chasing it too dearly.
