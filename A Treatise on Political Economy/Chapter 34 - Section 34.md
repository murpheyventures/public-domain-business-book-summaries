## Summary

The main charge against them is, that they buy up corn wdth the express purpose of raising its price, or at least of making an un- reasonable advantage upon the purchase and re- sale, which is ill effect so much gratuitous loss to the producer and consumer. Corn is a commodity of most extended production ; and its price cannot be arbitrarily raised, without disarming the compe- tition of an infinity of sellers, and without an extent of dealing and of agency scarcely prac- ticable to individuals. Legislation should take an enlarged view of commerce in the aggre- gate, small and great j it will find its agents busied in traversing the whole surface of the territory, watching every fluctuation of demand and supply, adjusting the casual or local deficiency of price to meet the charges of production, and excess of price above the capacity of consumption. 291 When the national government attempts to supply the population by becoming itself a dealer, it is sure to fail in satisfying the national wants itself and at the same time to extinguish all the resources, that freedom of commerce would offer ; for nobody else will knowingly embark in a losing trade, though the government may.
