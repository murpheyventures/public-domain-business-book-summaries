## Summary

There I was, once more broke, which was bad, and dead wrong in my trading, which was a sight worse. Having grown accustomed to swinging a big line--say, more than a hundred thousand shares of stock--I feared I would not show good judgment trading in a small way. After the habit of taking a big profit on a big line I wasn’t sure I would know when to take my profit on a small line. After all those long years of successes, tempered by mistakes that really served to pave the way for greater successes, I was now worse off than when I began in the bucket shops.
